There are several types of scales with different intervals:

  • Major Scale: 1t, 1t, 0.5t, 1t, 1t, 1t, 0.5t
  • Natural Minor Scale: 1t, 0.5t, 1t, 1t, 0.5t, 1t, 1t
  • Harmonic Minor Scale: 1t, 0.5t, 1t, 1t, 0.5t, 1.5t, 0.5t
  • Melodic Minor Scale Ascending: 1t, 0.5t, 1t, 1t, 1t, 1t, 0.5t
  • Melodic Minor Scale Descending: 1t, 0.5t, 1t, 1t, 0.5t, 1t, 1t
  • Chromatic Scale: 0.5t everywhere


Degrees of the Major Scale:

  • Tonic (I), Supertonic (II), Mediant (III), Subdominant (IV), Dominant (V), Superdominant (VI), Leading Tone (VII)

Major Scales:

  • C (-), G (1#), D (2#), A (3#), E (4#), B (5#), F# (6#), C# (7#)
  • C (-), F (1b), B♭ (2b), E♭ (3b), A♭ (4b), D♭ (5b), G♭ (6b), C (7b)

Minor Scales:

  • A (-), E (1#), B (2#), F# (3#), C# (4#), G# (5#), D# (6#), A# (7#)
  • A (-), D (1b), G (2b), C (3b), F (4b), B♭ (5b), E♭ (6b), A (7b)
