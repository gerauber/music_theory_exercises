There are several types of chords with three or four notes, and several inversions:

Three-note chords: (formed from a root, a third, and a fifth)

  • Diminished: 3m + 3m = 5d
  • Minor: 3m + 3M = 5J
  • Major: 3M + 3m = 5J
  • Augmented: 3M + 3M = 5a

Inversions of three-note chords:

  • Root Position: 3 + 3 = 5 (the root is in the bass)
  • First Inversion: 3 + 4 = 6 (the third is in the bass)
  • Second Inversion: 4 + 3 = 6 (the fifth is in the bass)

Four-note chords: (formed from a root, a third, a fifth, and a seventh)

  • Diminished: 3m + 3m + 3m = 7d
  • Dominant: 3M + 3m + 3m = 7m
